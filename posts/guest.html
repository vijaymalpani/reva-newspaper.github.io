BRINGING IN NEW PERSPECTIVE


The School of Arts and Humanities conducted a guest lecture on gender studies as a part of their curriculum from 7th January 2019 to 11th January 2019. The week long lecture was given by Dr. Elizabeth Joy Birmingham, Dean, Faculty of Social Sciences and Humanities and Dr. Anne Lorene Chambers, Chair Faculty of Social Sciences and Humanities, Lakehead University, Canada with whom REVA has a collaboration with.
The guest lecturers gave useful insight into the Canadian laws regarding women in relation to aspects like education, work, marriage etc. They spoke at length regarding the evolution of these laws and the famous cases that brought about these changes, like the case of O. J. Simpson v/s The State. "The lectures were an eye opener. It helped me to understand the reality of the western culture.", said Naveen K, one of the students who attended the lecture. It was an interactive session where students tried to relate these laws to the Indian scenario and the underlying problems that still exist like rape, honour killing, domestic violence and sexual harrasment to name a few. Students were given a chance to ask questions which helped clear any misconceptions and also gave them a new perspective.
