National Student Film Festival ‘Li & Le’


National Student Film Festival ‘Li & Le’ A Brief Report
About the Event
School of Arts and Humanities National Student Film Festival ‘Li & Le’.

National Student Film Festival ‘Li & Le’ was held at REVA University by The School Of Arts And Humanities. The 2nd day of the film festival consisted of various film related workshops by people from the industry. The festival started at 10:00 am with a workshop conducted by Mr. Pampally, Film Director and Screenwriter. This was followed by various other workshops by Mr. Ranjith, Mr Hemanth Rao, FIlm Director, Mr Niranjan Deshpande, Actor and Mr V. S. Kashinath, WIldlife Photographer. The workshops were followed by lunch at 1:45 pm and the festival resumed with participants taking part in various competitions like Shutterbug and Dubsmash/Tik Tok. The highlight of the event were the short films screened in Kuvempu Auditorium. Upto 50 submissions were narrowed down and judged by Mr. Pampally and Mr. Doddaiyah. The students participated with enthusiasm and won prizes for various competitions.
About REVA University REVA University is a private co-educational college in Bangalore, Karnataka, established in 2002, managed by Bheemaneni Educational Service Trust. In 2004 it was taken over by Rukmini Educational Charitable Trust led by Shri P. Shyamaraju.
It received an accreditation by NBA within a record time span of seven years post its inception in 2004 . All the institutions of the Trust are most sought after by aspiring students for pursuing higher education because of their excellent infrastructure, highly qualified faculty, commendable placement record, keen industry-institute interaction and various R&D initiatives.
REVA University, School of Arts and Humanities launched a National Student Film Festival called Li & Le. The 2 chief guests gracing the occasion were Director and Producer Mr Yograj Bhat and Director and Actor M. G. Srinivas along with Hon'ble Vice Chancellor S. Y. Kulkarni who presided over the inaugural ceremony and facilitated the guests.
The ceremony started with lighting of the lamp and an video of their profile. Shri Yograj Bhatt inaugurated the film festival by launching the 'Li & Le 'video. “Every movie comes from the heart and the mind of the writer”, he said. The inaugural ceremony was followed by an interactive session between students and the cast of upcoming film 'Panchatantra'. The ceremony ended with the screening of M. G. Srinivas's movie 'Birbal' which was well received by students and staff.

Short Film Screening


Short Film Screening
The Short Film screening of Li &Le REVA University National Film Festival began at 3:00 p.m. in Kuvempu Auditorium in the presence of Mr. Doddaiyah and Mr. Pampally as the jury members of the event. Upto 50 films were submitted which were then narrowed down. The first screened film was a Kannada short film called "Sex OTHER" depicting a story of a trans-woman in Bangalore, which was followed by "Captionless - no bound for emotion" a Malayalam short film which brought out the difference in between the childhood of the older generations and the present generation.
The third screened film, ‘Project Sweetlime’ is a’ Kannada movie which addressed the issue of social media addiction and the right usage of the same. The fourth movie screened was, ‘Jaalathraana’ a Kannada movie bringing out the issue of kidnapping and human trafficking, especially children.
"Ghatakan" a Malayalam movie was screened fifth which brought out the concept of death in a dual perspective. One as end of life and the other as the continuity after death
"Swara" was the next short film screened depicting the revenge of a ghost for the death of her child.
The last movie screened was "Auto-psy" featuring a 12 year old girl's rape and how a photographer sees it prior in her dream that she was going to be asked to cover that rape story.

Valedictory Report


Valedictory Report
The valedictory of ‘Li & Le’ film festival ’ was held at Kuvempu Auditorium, C. V. Raman Block. Which concluded the film festival by announcing the winners of the competitions and a vote of thanks to the guests, sponsors and students.
More than 30 registrations for the photography competition, made it tough for the judges to pick a winner. Hemanth was the winner of ‘Shutterbug’, competition.
The winner of Documentary film was Jayabharatan. In short film the first place was won by ‘Sex: Other’ by Anirudh Bhatt.’. In 2nd place was ‘Captionless: No Bound For Emotions’’ by Raghav and in 3rd place was ‘Two wheeler’ by Uttam Benedict. Deepshi was the winner of dubsmash competition.
The Sponsors of the festival Redhan Production House were thanked and Mr Inder Kumar's wife addressed the audience on his behalf. The reason they decided to sponsor the film festival was because of the changing trends in the industry. They wanted to focus on the youth and their perspective and focus on real issues existing in society.
The ceremony ended with a vote of thanks for the all the guests, participants, organisers and staff.
