School of Electronics and Communication Engineering hosts talk on “Leveraging Open Source Tools and Technology for System on Chip (SoC) Design”


The School of ECE organised a talk on “Leveraging Open Source Tools and Technology for System On Chip (SoC) Design” on 16th January 2019 from 8:30 a.m. to 9:35 a.m. in Aryabhatta Seminar Hall, C V Raman Block.

The resource person of the Technical Talk was Mr. Pragnajit Datta Roy, Senior Staff Engineer, Qualcomm, Bangalore, who has more than 17 years of experience in SoC performance modelling/analysis (SystemC), High-Level Synthesis design methodology, tools and validation. He is currently leading pre-silicon platform performance engineering and analysis work for Snapdragon SoC at Qualcomm Inc. His field of experience includes experience of IP and Ssystem performance optimization challenges and trade-off having led performance analysis work on multiple Snapdragon chipset catering to mid-tier and value-tier OEMs like Oppo, Vivo, Xiaomi etc. His areas of interest are High-Level Synthesis, Computer Architecture, Machine Vision, Persistent Memory and storage, CPU/GPU/Memory/System performance analysis.

Around 70 students from the School of ECE attended the Technical Talk.
The Technical Talk focused on following key points:
1. What is SoC?
2. SoC Evolution: Moore’s Law
3. SoC Evolution: From Single Core to MultiCore
4. SoC Evolution: From CISC to RISC
5. SoC Evolution: From Proprietary to Open Source tools and ISA
6. SoC Evolution: Open Source SoC for industry and Academia
7. New Business Model: SoC Design on Cloud
8. How REVA can use open source ecosystem to design its own SoC chip.
