Memoirs of a Geisha

"if you aren't the women I think you are then this isn't the world I thought it was"
Memoirs of a Geisha
Our protagonist Chiyo , a 11 year old girl, is engulfed by a wave and thrown into an entirely different world in the prime of her childhood. The story begins in yoroido where Chiyo is a poor fisherman's daughter.
When Chiyo is sold to an okiya (house for Geisha)in Gion she is expected to learn the arts of becoming a Geisha and pay back her debts including the money the okiya paid to buy her, soon earn for the okiya.
A Geisha spends her time mastering the art of conversing and applying the correct strategy to become associated with a Danna ( a man who pays for the Geisha's company).She spends hours trying to perfect her makeup and her skills in various artforms and Tea ceromonies.
Our visual senses are awakened by the stunning discriptions of every kimono Chiyo ever wore or saw, which are so important in understanding the culture.
The author has described the culture of Gion with such detail and intricacy. we can almost hear the high pitched laughter of Geisha and their fast chatter.
Throughout my read , I was accompanied by a feeling of indignation. Through Chiyo's illuminating desrciptions of Kimonos, the art of applying makeup, the various stages of apprenticeship before one gains the status of Geisha and the flattering admirers , the hurdles a Geisha had to face to gain perfection,daily remarks thrown at them and the fact that some of them were sold into the career,certainly fueled my feminism -Nisha Anil Kumar
