“Guest Lecture on “Cancers in Women and its awareness””



About the Event
Guest Lecture on “Cancers in Women and its awareness”

Date: 7/03/19
Time: 11:20 PM to 02:00 PM

School:
School of Applied Sciences

Venue:
Library Seminar Hall, REVA University

Description of Event:
On account of International Women’s Day, The Department of Biochemistry, School of Applied sciences organized a Guest Lecture on “Cancers in Women and its awareness” under Bioquimica forum on 7/03/19 at Library Seminar Hall, REVA University. Resource Person was Dr Ajay Rao P, Chief consultant Radiology oncologist, Cytecare Cancer Hospitals. Around 150 female students and Faculty members were a part of this Guest Lecture. The most common types of cancer in women were discussed and some of the misconceptions in the subject was clarified. In a nutshell, it was a very informative lecture that we received on this women’s day. In continuation to this, screening and mammogram test was organized for the female staff of our university in association with Cytecare cancer hospitals under Unnathi Women’s forum on 8th March 2019. Around 40 female staff underwent the screening process as a self-care for this women’s day.

Some Snappets of Event









DEPARTMENT OF CHEMISTRY


As a part of Science Appreciation Club activity, we the Department of Chemistry in association with Department of Biochemistry and Biotechnology,School of Applied Sciences, REVA University, Bangalore organized an Industry Expert R&D Lectureon “Newer Trends of Drug Discovery and Enzyme Biotherapeutics” on 11th March 2019 at 9 AM in the Seminar Hall-1, Basement, Vivekananda Block, REVA University for the students of MSc., Chemistry, Biochemistry and Biotechnology, Research scholars and Staff.
The expert lecture was delivered by:
Dr. Ramesh Babu Boga
PhD President & CEO Boga RLaboratoties LLC, USA.

The Expert lecture focused on the following key points:

  1. Challenging tasks encountered during the development of new drugs for various new diseases
  2. New Chemical Entities (NCEs) – origin, structure determination and synthesis
  3. Hi Tech Security Office – REVA University main gate
  4. DNA-encoded chemical libraries and their utilities
  5. Application of Enzyme Technology for new drug discovery in pharma and biotechnology

The talk follows interactive discussions and Q&A.

Following are the outcomes achieved:

  • Understanding the importance of organic/medicinal/biochemistry in pharma
  • Learning newer chemical and biomolecular approaches in drug discovery
  • Application of enzymes as biocatalysts
  • Understanding the applications of DNA based chemical libraries
  • Identification of disease targets are based on the enzymes, and enzymology
  • The students explored to new innovations in the drug discovery program
  • Importance of enzymology and enzyme technology

Then session concluded with encouraging thoughts and Dr. Babu also shared opportunities in his company in India and USA.
