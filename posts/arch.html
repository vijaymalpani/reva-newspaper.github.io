REVA University: Programs conducted by School of Architecture


Rural Documentation Tour to Barkur and Brahmavar for 4th semester B. Arch students on 18-19th January 2019.
The purpose of the trip was to gain an understanding of vernacular architecture in the coastal belt of Karnataka with reference to socio cultural context, planning, architectural character, climate, material availability and construction techniques. . Faculty members Mrs. Neeraja Jayan, Mr. Sanjeev Koppar, Mr. Murali J, Mr. Vidya Srikanth accompanied the students on the tour. The students got an insight into the visual character of both locations Barkur and Brahmavar, understanding the surroundings and history related to Barkur and the unique vernacular techniques of constructions followed in the different house typologies.

Case study visit to IIM Bangalore for 6th semester B.Arch students on 25th January 2019.
The purpose of the visit was for students to study different aspects of campus planning. The IIMB campus was designed by Pritzker Prize winner architect B V Doshi. . Mr Shama Parween, Ms. Kiruthika Selvi, Mr Nazish Abid accompanied the students on the case study visit. The campus architecture of the academic and administrative blocks became the case study. The visit helped students in understanding the various planning aspects to be considered while designing an institutional campus and integration of various built and un-built spaces.

Case study visit to SJBIT, Kengeri for 6th semester B.Arch students
This visit was organized as a part of curriculum for students of Architecture which requires students to study different aspects of campus planning on a contoured site. The SJBIT campus was selected as it is located on a contoured site with level differences of almost 5-6m. The students were asked to draw sections across the main spine road at different points, and analyze the impact of contours in planning of individual block.

Skill Development Program on Digital 3D making tricks organized by School of Architecture from Jan 31st to 3rd Feb 2019.
A skill development program was organized for 6th sem students. Three dimensional digital models are the best way to demonstrate architectural design.. The resource person, Mr. Ujjal Halder demonstrated various techniques and tricks which can help students deliver good quality drawings within a short span of time. However, making digital 3D model is quite a complicated process and time consuming process.

Skill Development Program on Wood cut print by School of Architecture
School of Architecture faculty member Anupama R M. conducted a 3 days Skill development Program – “Wood-cut print” from 8-10th Feb 2019 for the 1st year students of architecture. Students got an opportunity to explore wood by cutting, carving and translating it into an art piece which could be helpful in various interior works. Around 68 students enthusiastically participated in the workshop.

“INQUIZITIVE” architecture quiz organized by LITWITS-School of Architecture Literary Club.
A quiz on Architecture and allied knowledge base was organized by REVA University on 7th February 2019. Around 60 students from across all semesters participated enthusiastically in the hour long quiz. Quiz enthusiasts were divided into groups of 3 each and they attempted a total of 30 questions on a wide range of topics related to art and architecture. The students participated and thoroughly enjoyed the quiz.

Site Visit to Chikmaglur for B.Arch 6th Semester students
A visit to Chikmagalur was organized as a part of curriculum for 6th semester students of Architecture from 8th to 10th February 2019. A site has been selected in Chikmagalur near Rameshwara lake, where students worked on design proposals for Campus Planning in the currents semester. Students were taken to the site to study various aspects like Surrounding, history, topography, climatic condition, sensory features, local culture and architecture. Students also visited the Belur and Halebidu to study the rock cut architecture of Hoysala dynasty. Faculty members Mr. Shama p, Mr. Nazish A. and Ms. Kiruthika S. accompanied the students for the site visit.

Faculty Acheivments from Jan 10 2019 to 14 Feb 2019
School of Architecture Faculty members attend Council of Architecture Teachers Training Program at Pune. Mr. Vidya Srikanth and Mr. Shubhi S from School of Architecture were nominated to attend Council of Architecture Teachers Training program on Advanced Architectural theories from 28th Jan-1st Feb 2019 at COA-TRC, Pune. The intensive training session involved lecture sessions by renowned architects such as Mr. Pradnya Chouhan, Mr. Durganand Balsavar, Mr. Chethan Sahasrabudhe, Mr Kaber Fatema and Mr. Jayshree Deshpande. The program concluded with group wise preparation of book manuals which can be used as easy to comprehend visual tools in the design studios across semesters. The book manuals prepared by the participants during the 5 day program are now under review for publication by COA-TRC and will be shared to Architecture schools across India after publication. School of Architecture Faculty member selected for International workshop in Bali and China Mr. Yashaswini N., Asst. professor at School of Architecture was selected to participate in a 12 days architecture workshop titled-“The Spatial intensive”. The program explored engineering and artistic environment in Asia through guided visits to Bali and China. The program included visits to award winning projects, landmarks, workshops, curated walks and also a conscious effort to understand and explore the mixture of art and craft with modern architecture. School of Architecture faculty member presents paper at International conference at Ireland Mr. Anup Kumar Prasad successfully presented research paper on Significance of Indo-Saracenic Architecture in India during British Raj at All Ireland Architectural Research Group 8, hosted by Dublin School of Architecture, Dublin, Ireland, on 24th and 25th January 2019.
